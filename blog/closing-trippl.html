Closing a Chapter: Two Years of Trippl

February 2026

I closed the chapter on Trippl recently. Two and a half years, a team of seven, a product I built from scratch — and then it was time to move on.

Trippl started as Get Home Safe. Women shouldn't feel unsafe getting home at night. We built a ride-sharing marketplace matching people on the same route at the same time. I was CTO. I built everything — iOS, Android, dashboard, website, backend.

The hardest thing about a marketplace: both sides need to show up at the same time. No drivers, riders leave. No riders, drivers leave. Months of chicken-and-egg — subsidized rides, campus ambassadors, student union partnerships. Some worked, most didn't.

What I actually learned isn't "marketplaces are hard." It's that human coordination is a software problem. When it worked — 200 riders matched on the same night, all arriving and departing on schedule — it was beautiful. The challenge wasn't the algorithm. It was getting hundreds of people to do the same thing at the same time, reliably.

That insight is the foundation of everything I'm building now at 8x. Different domain — creators instead of riders, content instead of routes — but the same core problem. Coordinate humans across geographies, keep quality consistent, pay them fairly, scale without scaling headcount linearly.

I was 21 when we started. Never managed anyone. Suddenly running standups, code reviews, roadmap decisions, team conflicts — while trying to ship features. Learned the CTO job is 30% writing code and 70% making sure other people can write code effectively.

When I decided to step away, I didn't hesitate. Not because I was tired. Because I could see the next thing I wanted to build was bigger, and Trippl wasn't going to get me there.

Don't regret a day of it. Onto the next one.
